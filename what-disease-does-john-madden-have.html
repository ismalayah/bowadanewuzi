What disease does John Madden have?

Publish date: 2024-07-21

John Madden is an American former football coach and sportscaster. The legendary American football icon won Super Bowl XI as head coach of the Oakland Raiders over the Minnesota Vikings.

John Madden after retiring from coaching became a well-known color commentator for National Football League telecasts. He was born on April 10, 1936, in Austin, Minnesota.

In 2006, he was inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame in recognition of his coaching career.

John Madden disease

What disease does John Madden have? John Madden is suffering from heart-related disease. The NFL Communication confirmed that he has undergone open heart surgery.

“Pro Football Hall of Fame Coach John Madden recently underwent successful open-heart surgery to repair blockages. He was released from a San Francisco hospital yesterday and is expected to fully recover.

“I spoke to John yesterday,” NFL Commissioner Roger Goodell said. “He sounds great, but is frustrated about being on injured reserve for the time being. He is anxious to return to action soon doing the important work he does for our game.

‘He’s a tremendous fountain of ideas and we can’t wait to get him back. We are all pleased to know that John’s procedure was successful and wish him a speedy recovery.”

‘Madden serves as co-chair with Ronnie Lott of the NFL Player Safety Advisory Panel and is chair of the Coaches Subcommittee to the Competition Committee.”
